The chokepoint

Value moves east at the smelter gate

Western miners dig it up. The economic and strategic leverage — the value-added, the processing know-how, the supply chain control — lands in Asia. China rarely leads in mining. In refining, it dominates.

Key takeaways — refined production

  • China rarely leads in mining — but dominates refining for graphite, cobalt, rare earths, lithium, copper
  • Alignment shifts dramatically downstream: mined is mostly Western; refined is increasingly China and Russia
  • Western ESG and capital flows into mining — supply chain risk sits downstream, where Western capacity has atrophied
  • Production headlines distract. Refining is the real chokepoint.
  • Uranium is the outlier: Russia dominates enrichment, not China — a distinct strategic risk profile
30 years of evidence · 10 years of projection

This isn't a snapshot. It's a trajectory.

The alignment shift didn't happen overnight. It was built — deliberately, systematically — over three decades of Chinese refinery investment during the commodity supercycle. The question now is whether the projected partial rebalancing (IRA, Western refinery investment) arrives fast enough to matter.

What the animation shows

  • 1994: Most commodities sit near neutral or Western-aligned — refining gaps are modest
  • 2000–2012: The China supercycle — refined bubbles migrate right at speed, gaps widen dramatically
  • 2014: Russia's alignment shift is visible — uranium enrichment becomes a separate risk axis
  • 2024: Gaps are structural, not cyclical — 30 years of consistent directional movement
  • 2025–2035 (projected): IRA and Western refinery investment show partial rebalancing for lithium, rare earths, uranium — but graphite barely moves
The investment and policy implication

We are fixing the wrong bottleneck

Capital, permitting effort, and ESG attention flow overwhelmingly into mining — the upstream end of the value chain that is already reasonably well distributed geographically. The downstream chokepoint, where strategic leverage actually resides, receives a fraction of that attention.

Mining is noise
Geography of ore extraction is broadly distributed and improving
Refining is signal
Processing concentration has been tightening for 30 years and remains highly asymmetric
The gap is the thesis
The distance between the hollow and solid circles — commodity by commodity — is the investment opportunity and the policy problem

Not new news — but visualised through a Tufte lens, the implications are clearer. Verticalisation of supply chains is topical precisely because this data has been hiding in plain sight. The country that controls the smelter controls the price, the timing, and ultimately the strategic option.
